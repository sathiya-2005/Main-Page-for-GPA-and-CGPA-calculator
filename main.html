What is GPA?

‣ GPA stands for Grade Point Average. It is a numerical representation of a student's academic performance based on the grades they have achieved in their courses.
‣ GPA is typically calculated on a scale of 0 to 10.0 in the Indian education system, with 10.0 being the highest possible GPA. The GPA scale may vary in different countries.
‣ Here is a simple formula to calculate GPA:
‣ Assign a numerical value to each letter grade. For example:
S = 10.0
A = 9.0
B = 8.0
C = 7.0
D = 6.0
F = 5.0
‣ Multiply the numerical value of each grade by the number of credits for the course.
‣ Add up all the resulting values for each course.
‣ Divide the total value by the total number of credits taken. This will give you the GPA.

What is CGPA?

‣ CGPA, which stands for Cumulative Grade Point Average, is a crucial measure of a student's academic performance throughout their academic journey. When considering a grading scale of 10, the highest achievable grade, often a S, would be associated with the highest point value, such as 10. As the grades decrease, the corresponding point values also decrease.
‣ The CGPA is calculated by averaging the accumulated points earned in all subjects or courses and then scaling it to fit within the 10-point scale. This provides a comprehensive representation of a student's academic performance over time.
‣ CGPA offers a standardized way to measure and compare academic performance across different students, classes, and schools. It's a crucial metric for students, teachers, and institutions to understand a student's overall academic standing.
‣ Understanding the CGPA on a 10-point scale is essential for both students and educational institutions, providing a holistic view of academic achievement. If you're aiming for a clear understanding of your academic journey, the CGPA is a key indicator to focus on.
